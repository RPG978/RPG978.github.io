## Technical Competencies Across Core Computer Science Domains
The Computer Science program provided a comprehensive foundation in the technical competencies essential to modern software development. Through coursework and applied projects, I developed a strong command of data structures and algorithms, software engineering principles, database design, and security practices. These domains collectively shaped my ability to design efficient systems, reason about computational complexity, and implement solutions that are both strong and sustainable.

A significant portion of my academic development centered on data structures and algorithms, which formed the analytical core of the program. Through continued algorithmic problem solving, I learned to evaluate trade offs in time and space complexity, select appropriate data structures for specific use cases, and apply algorithmic strategies such as recursion, dynamic programming, and graph traversal. These experiences strengthened my ability to approach problems systematically and to justify design decisions based on computational efficiency and theoretical grounding.

Software engineering coursework further expanded my understanding of how to translate conceptual designs into functional, scalable systems. I gained experience with modular architecture, object oriented design, version control, and iterative development methodologies. These courses emphasized the importance of writing clean, maintainable code and adhering to established design patterns. Additionally, database courses introduced me to relational modeling, SQL, normalization, and the principles of reliable data management. Together, these competencies enabled me to design systems that integrate application logic with structured data storage in a coherent and efficient manner.

Security principles were consistently woven within the curriculum, reinforcing the importance of building systems that are resilient to misuse and vulnerabilities. I developed an understanding of secure coding practices, input validation, authentication and authorization concepts, and common attack vectors. These lessons emphasized that security is not an isolated concern but a fundamental aspect of responsible software development. Incorporating these principles into my work has strengthened my ability to design systems that prioritize both functionality and protection of user data.

Collectively, these technical competencies form the foundation of my professional skill set. They have equipped me with the theoretical knowledge and practical experience necessary to approach complex problems with confidence, design effective solutions, and contribute meaningfully to the development of reliable, secure software systems.
